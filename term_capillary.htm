capillary (m)

Variable in the module Quinton.  Capillary water is the depth of water (m) held in an HRU layer by surface tension as calculated from the Van Genuchten expression.  It is used to determine the maximum amount of water that can be held in an HRU layer.  Any excess is transferred to layerwater.

It is assumed that when the soil tension is less than the bubbling pressure the soil drains by gravity.

          q f = (f - q r )*(2n )-m + q r   where y b = 1/a

Above the bubbling pressure the volumetric soil moisture is

q = (f - q r )*((1 + ( a|y|)n )-m + q r

where q = volumetric soil moisture content,

f =  porosity or volume of soil pores,

y = soil moisture tension (m) equal to Depth -

n = constant,

m = 1 - 1/n

a =  a*m - 1, a =  25 for above 0.1 m and a =  15 for greater depths.
